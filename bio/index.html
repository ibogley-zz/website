My Background

I grew up in Corvallis, Oregon, hometown of Oregon State University. Through the years, I spent a lot of time on various fields playing sports like baseball, soccer, and ultimate frisbee.

My college career actually began at OSU, but I transfered to the University of Oregon at the end of my Freshman year. Since the switch, I switched majors from Mathematics to Economics and ended up becoming a graduate student as well.

Graduating this December with a Master's degree in Applied Economics at the U of O, I have spent my time focusing on my passion: clear communication of information in the pursuit of improved problem-solving.

There are a lot of problems out there. As an American I believe our finest attribute is the spirit of compromise and curiousity with which we operate. We want information so that we can solve problems, in the hopes that if we keep solving them one-by-one, we can improve the lives of ourselves and others through voluntary cooperation. I look forward to the opportunity to add my name to this work.
